Our Cabin Crew Simulator Solutions



Our Initial Cabin Crew Safety Procedure Training takes place in a Virtual Realistic Aviation Environment. Here, participants acquire comprehensive knowledge and practical basic knowledge in order to be able to safely fulfill their tasks and responsibilities as cabin crew for the safety of passengers in a normal, exceptional or emergency situation.

Training content
  • 1. Safety and emergency procedures (SEP)
    • a. General theoretical knowledge of aviation
    • b. Communication
    • c. General theoretical knowledge of regulations within aviation
    • d. Dealing with passengers
    • e. Fire and smoke training, survival training and emergency watering exercise
    • f. Aviation security
  • 2. Crew Resource Management (CRM)
  • 3. First aid
  • 4. Dangerous goods regulations

As part of Aircraft Type Specific Training, flight attendants will learn the specific procedures of the airline and the respective aircraft type. This gives them an insight into the environment of the machine and gets to know the performance of the pattern-specific systems and equipment. The aim is for all cabin crew members to be able to perform their duties on board according to the established procedures.

Training content
  • 1. Safety and emergency procedures (SEP)
  • 2. Crew Resource Management (CRM)
  • 3. First aid
  • 4. Aviation Security





The aim of the recurrent training is to train and check whether the cabin crew members are able to act correctly on board the aircraft type assigned to them. In both normal and emergency situations, flight attendants should always be able to act in accordance with the operator manual.
